Tomatoes

Tomatoes are planted for their red fleshed fruits when ripe. Tomatoes have a variety of uses in cooking soups, making salads and even in the manufacture of tomato paste or sauces among other uses. The common markets for tomatoes include Mbare Musika Market , Food Lovers and other major supermarkets, restaurants and tomato sauce manufacturers.


Tomatoes are best transplanted from seedbeds or trays to the field ,6-8weeks after emergence. Seedlings can be hardened when they have 3-5 true leaves and are pencil thick or approximately 12 cm tall. Planting is done using 300 mm to 500 mm in row and 1.5 – 2 m between rows spacing.


Apply basal dressing of Compound S 1 000– 1 500 kg/ha (6:17:6), or Tobacco fert (6:15:12) at 700 kg/ha Broadcast half and disc into the soil then apply the other half at planting .Apply top dressing of Ammonium Nitrate and Potassium Sulphate each at 100 kg/ha, split into 2 or 3 applications. Yields of over 100t/ha can be obtained depending on cultural practices and cultivars. However a yield range of 20-60 t/ha is usually achievable.

Varieties

Heinz 1370 (Klein Karoo)

It is an open field variety which matures in 80-90days. Fruits are deep oblate to globe in shape. Fruit mass averages between 120-150g and possesses a fair shelf life.

Tengeru 97 ( Royal Seedlings)

Determinate tomato variety (has definite flowering and fruiting).Fruits are firm with very good red color both internal and external color. A mature fruit weighs between 90-120g
