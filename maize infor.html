Maize (Chibage/Umumbu)

Maize is the staple product for Zimbabwe which is commonly used for mealie meal production. Common markets for maize include the Grain Marketing Board, Irvines among other stock feed producers and millers. There are two major groups of cultivars:
  • Hybrids whose seeds cannot be recycled
  • Open-pollinated varieties, whose seed can be recycled for at least 3 seasons before buying

When planting maize an average of 25kg /ha is recommended at a rate of 45 000plants /ha( 90 cm x 25 cm) in highrainfall areas, 37 000 plants/ha ( 90 cm x 30 cm) for low rainfall areas and 22000 plant/ha (1.5m x 30 cm) on tied ridges for dry areas . Planting can be done 2-3 weeks prior to the first effective rain or alternatively plant with first effective rains (dry planting), or with irrigation in mid-October before the rain.

Fertilizer requirement is between 150-350kg/ha of Compound D and 100-300kg od Ammonium nitrate as dependent on soil test results. Generally, the whole of P & K requirements and at least 1/3 N should be applied at planting by either broadcasting or banding in planting rows. The top dressing fertilizer (AN) is recommended at 4-6 weeks after planting or at maize knee height if applied once. IIf topdressing is split because of high rainfall, the first 1/3 can be applied at 4-6 weeks and the last 1/3 at 8 weeks after maize planting.

Varieties

SC 633 (Seedco)

Very high white coloured grain and has good grading and baking qualities. It is resistant to lodging but medium to tall in height.
It is immune to stem rust, moderate resistant to leaf rust and powdery mildew. Have high field resilience.

PAN 7M-81 (Pannar)

A new medium maturity hybrid which is a top yielder, coupled with leaf disease resistance. The multi-cobber is suited to irrigation, exhibits good standability as well as Sulphonyl urea tolerance and a perfect choice for double cropping.

KKS 4555 (Klein Karoo)

A hybrid variety which takes 120-134 days to maturity.
