Soyabeans

Soya beans are a very good cash crop and rotational break crop for maize, sorghum, millet, wheat, etc. Soya has a number of uses and therefore in demand by cooking oil manufactures , stock feeds manufactures as well as those who produce soyabeans by products like soya mince.


The ideal population is 300,000 plants/ha (germinated and emerged). To get this, plant a total of 400,000 seeds/Ha. This means that you must carefully dribble 30-33 seeds per metre into the bottom of the furrow.

Try and space these seeds as evenly as your can about 3 to 3.3cm apart in the furrow. Do the best you can, but do not be too slow otherwise you may miss your rainfall opportunity and the soil may get too dry to plant.

It is very important to plant your soya seeds shallow at a depth of between 1cm and 2cm (the thickness of a matchbox). It is better to plant at ½ cm than 3 cm, unless you have very soft soil with lots of mulch.

Soyas respond very well to residual fertiliser (fertiliser applied to previous crops) Soya beans is an easy crop to grow except that there are two critical operations that must be done at a very high standard.
